What is LAISS?

The Liberal Arts Initiative

LAISS (Liberal Arts Initiative Summer School) is a non-profit program organized by students from Amherst College and taught by professors from Amherst College. The program offers classes of multiple disciplines as well as writing workshops and information sessions.

Our Aims

  1. Provide first-hand experience of liberal arts education to high school students in China
  2. Promote better understanding of liberal arts education among future Chinese intellectuals
  3. Inspire academic thoughts and researches in different academic fields among Chinese high school students and Amherst professors.
  4. Serve as an affordable and high-quality platform of communication between outstanding Chinese students and US professors/students.

Amherst College

Amherst College is the most prestigious liberal arts college in the United States. It is located in Amherst, Massachusetts, an area that has abundant top-notch education resources. Amherst College has 1,785 students (as of November 2013), more than 200 faculty members and more than 2,1500 active alumni. Amherst College offers the Bachelor of Arts degree in 38 majors in the arts, sciences, social sciences and humanities. Students can carry more than one major, create their own interdisciplinary major or engage in independent scholarship. With more than 115 student organizations, Amherst College provides the students with the best educational experience and extra-curricular life. Amherst College is one of the only six colleges (and the only liberal arts college) across the country that provide need-based financial aid to international students, as the college seeks to create equal and affordable college education to the most aspiring students, regardless of their national and financial background. About 60 percent of the total student body receive sufficient financial aid for demonstrated need. The average aid for students is $46,809. At the same time, Amherst College faculty, who are national leaders of different academic areas, are of the most highly paid among the country’s scholars. Amherst College alumni, who become leaders of diverse industries and social departments, have endowed the college with $1.824 billion (as of June 30, 2013).

Ranked as the best national liberal arts college for ten times by U.S. News education, Amherst College is one of the most rigorous higher education institutions. Significant alumni of Amherst College include John Calvin Coolidge, 30th president of U.S., and Dan Brown, renown fiction writer and author of Da Vinci Code. Renown faculty members include Davide Hall, who recently published a most influential article on the creation of synthetic magnetic particle, and Xiaonian Xu, a most influential economist in China.

Curriculum

The LAISS 2014 Curriculum consists of four academic courses and one writing workshop. Students will participate in two courses according to their indicated preference and the writing workshop. All of the courses include lectures and liberal arts style small group discussions. Writing workshops will be held in small groups and students will work closely with the professor.

Latin America’s Role in the Global Economy and the Rise of BRICs
by Professor Corrales

Political Ideas and Practices in the United States
by Professor Dumm

In this course we will study some of the most important political thinkers and political documents that have shaped the American understanding and practices of politics, from the American Founding through the present. Of particular focus will be on the evolving relationship of freedom to democracy, the impact that practices of slavery and free labor have had on political development, and the emergence of American empire in the 20th century.

Money, Meaning, and Morality
by Professor Gentzler

What sorts of things should we be able to buy?  Does putting a high price on certain goods– e.g., education, political access, children– not only prevent those with less money from acquiring these important human goods, but also diminish the sorts of value that we are able to recognize and appreciate?  Through an extended reading of Michael Sandel’s What Money Can’t Buy: The Moral Limits of Markets, we will reflect on, discuss, and write extensively about what has value and why.  

Writing Workshop
by Professor Gentzler

Our Faculty

Javier Corrales

PhD, Harvard University
Department of Political Science, Amherst College

Javier Corrales is professor of Political Science at Amherst College in Amherst, Massachusetts.  He obtained his Ph.D. in political science from Harvard University.   He is the co-author with Daniel Altschuler of The Promise of Participation: Experiments in Participatory Governance in Honduras and Guatemala (Palgrave/Macmillan 2013), with Carlos A. Romero of U.S.-Venezuela Relations since the 1990s:  Coping with Midlevel Security Threats (Routledge, 2013), and with Michael Penfold of Dragon in the Tropics:  Hugo Chávez and the Political Economy of Revolution in Venezuela (Brookings Institution Press, 2011).   His research has been published in academic journals such as Comparative Politics, World Development, Political Science Quarterly, International Studies Quarterly, World Policy Journal, Latin American Politics and Society, Journal of Democracy, Latin American Research Review, Studies in Comparative International Studies, Current History, and Foreign Policy. He is also working on a book manuscript on constitutional reforms in Latin America.  At Amherst, he teaches courses on Comparative Politics, Latin American Politics, International Relations, and Political Economy.  As someone who draws on Asia for both his courses and research, he looks forward to spending visiting China and meeting Chinese students this summer.

Thomas Dumm

PhD, Cornell University
Department of Political Science, Amherst College

Thomas L. Dumm is the William H. Hastie ’25 Professor of Political Ethics at Amherst College. He is the author of six books in political philosophy, and co-editor of two other volumes. He is also a founding editor of the internationally known journal of contemporary political theory, Theory&Event. He specializes in American political thought and critical theory. His most recent book, Loneliness as a Way of Life (Harvard UP, 2008) received international acclaim, and has been translated into Dutch, Italian, and Korean. He new book, My Father’s House: On Will Barnet’s Paintings, will be released later this year by Duke University Press.

When asked if he was interested in teaching in China, he was delighted. Because of the critical importance of China’s role in shaping the world’s future, an opportunity to interact with young Chinese students in China is an opportunity too good to pass up. He is also very pleased that the course will be offered in Shanghai, which may be considered the world capital of the 21st century.

Jyl Gentzler

PhD, Cornell University
Department of Philosophy, Department of Black Studies, and Writing Center, Amherst College

Jyl Gentzler is William R. Kenan, Jr. Professor of Philosophy and Associate Director of the Writing Center at Amherst College.  Her area of specialization is Ancient Greek Philosophy with an emphasis on Socrates’, Plato’s, and Aristotle’s conceptions of a good human life.  Against the suggestion that ancient texts that have no relevance to modern life, she argues that we would benefit from taking seriously their insights.  She came to Amherst College in 1989, having studied at Bryn Mawr College (AB) and Cornell University (MA, PhD).

2014 Location, Schedule and Tuition

LAISS 2014 will be held in Shanghai New Oriental School, from Aug.16 to Aug.27. Comprehensive fee for LAISS 2014 is 12,000 RMB.
Students will commute to campus to participate classes, discussions, workshops, faculty office hours and activities. Non-local students will be assisted in finding accommodation in Shanghai.

Application

Applicants are required to fill out the application form and submit their TOEFL, SAT and SAT2, AP and IB scores, if they have any. Screen shots of the scores should be sent along with the form to laiss.application@gmail.com Test scores are not mandatory, but applicants are encouraged to demonstrate their academic abilities in the application.

Admission will be on a rolling basis. Spots are limited. After an applicant submits the application form, he/she will receive a confirmation stating his application status. If selected, he/she will be contacted for an interview as soon as possible (no later than a month). After the interview, we will notify him/her about the admission decision within two weeks and we will take down the class preference.

Applying to LAISS 2014 is free! Applicants selected for interviews will be asked charged a non-refundable fee of 200 RMB, which will be deducted from the comprehensive fee if the applicant is admitted.

Procedures of submitting the comprehensive fee will be posted shortly.
